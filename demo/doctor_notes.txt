with early pulmonary oedema. GRACE Score: 148 (High Risk for in-hospital

FINAL DIAGNOSIS: NSTEMI — High Risk (GRACE ≥140)

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TREATMENT PLAN — EVIDENCE-BASED (ACC/AHA NSTEMI GUIDELINES)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. ANTIPLATELET THERAPY — DUAL (DAPT):
   - Aspirin 325mg STAT (loading dose, patient already self-administered — confirmed)
   - Aspirin 75mg OD (maintenance — continue for life)
   - Clopidogrel 600mg STAT (loading dose — P2Y12 inhibitor)
   - Clopidogrel 75mg OD (maintenance — 12 months minimum)
   Rationale: DAPT reduces platelet aggregation and prevents further coronary
   thrombus propagation in NSTEMI. Clopidogrel preferred over Ticagrelor given
   elevated bleeding risk (CKD Stage 2, age >50).

2. ANTICOAGULATION:
   - Unfractionated Heparin (UFH) infusion — weight-based protocol (60 U/kg bolus,
     then 12 U/kg/hr; max bolus 4000 U, max infusion 1000 U/hr)
   - Monitor aPTT every 6 hours — target 60–80 seconds
   - Continue until coronary angiography or 48 hours
   Rationale: UFH prevents further thrombus formation and maintains coronary patency
   while the patient is prepared for invasive strategy.

3. BETA-BLOCKER THERAPY:
   - Metoprolol Succinate 25mg BD (oral)
   - Titrate to HR 55–65 bpm and BP control
   - Hold if HR <50, SBP <90, or evidence of acute decompensation
   Rationale: Beta-blockade reduces myocardial oxygen demand, limits infarct size,
   and reduces risk of malignant arrhythmias in the acute MI setting.

4. HIGH-INTENSITY STATIN THERAPY:
   - Atorvastatin 80mg OD (at bedtime)
   - Target LDL <70 mg/dL (high-risk ACS target per ACC/AHA)
   Rationale: High-intensity statins stabilize atherosclerotic plaques, reduce
   inflammation (CRP), and have pleiotropic cardioprotective effects.

5. GLYCAEMIC CONTROL:
   - Hold Metformin (risk of contrast-induced nephropathy pre-angiography)
   - Sliding scale insulin — Regular Insulin per blood glucose (BG) protocol
   - BG Target: 140–180 mg/dL during acute admission (avoid tight control)
   - Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) in CCU
   Rationale: Hyperglycaemia worsens ischaemic outcomes. However, hypoglycaemia
   is equally harmful in acute MI — moderate target range is evidence-based.

6. OXYGEN THERAPY:
   - SpO2 currently 94% on room air — supplement with O2 via nasal cannula 2L/min
   - Target SpO2 ≥95% — titrate and discontinue when stable
   Note: Avoid hyperoxia (SpO2 >99%) — may worsen coronary vasoconstriction.

7. ACE INHIBITOR (INITIATE AFTER STABILISATION):
   - Ramipril 2.5mg OD — start once BP stable and creatinine checked post-procedure
   - Titrate to 10mg OD for cardioprotective effect
   Rationale: ACE inhibitors prevent LV remodelling post-MI, reduce afterload,
   and are indicated in patients with HTN, DM, and reduced LVEF.

8. LOOP DIURETIC:
   - IV Furosemide 40mg STAT — for pulmonary oedema (CXR-confirmed)
   - Monitor urine output, electrolytes (K+, Na+) q6h
   - Oral Furosemide 20mg OD after IV stabilization if needed

PROCEDURAL PLAN
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A. URGENT ECHOCARDIOGRAM — Within 4 hours
   - Assess LVEF (critical for guiding therapy and prognosis)
   - Rule out mechanical complications: VSD, MR, tamponade
   - Wall motion abnormality mapping to guide culprit vessel identification

B. CORONARY ANGIOGRAPHY (CAG) — Within 24 hours (Early Invasive Strategy)
   - GRACE ≥140 → Early Invasive Strategy mandated (ACC/AHA 2021 Class I)
   - Radial artery access preferred (lower bleeding risk)
   - Pre-procedural: IV Heparin, check renal function, HbA1c, INR
   - Hold Metformin 48h post-contrast

C. PERCUTANEOUS CORONARY INTERVENTION (PCI) — if CAG reveals culprit lesion
   - Drug-Eluting Stent (DES) preferred
   - Post-PCI: Continue DAPT (Aspirin + Clopidogrel for 12 months)
   - Glycoprotein IIb/IIIa inhibitor (Tirofiban) — at operator discretion
     if large thrombus burden or incomplete flow post-PCI
